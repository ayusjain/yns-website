## About This Episode

Aparna has been many things. A Chartered Accountant. A lawyer. A counsellor. A Montessori educator. A carpenter.

Not in parallel. In sequence. Each life complete before the next one began.

In 2020, she built a Montessori school — Hastam — from the ground up. It opened its doors into a global pandemic. When the world locked down, she didn't close. She picked up a drill and a chisel and built physical learning kits by hand. Her son taught her carpentry.

This is a story about not knowing who you are for a long time — and then finding out.

## What We Talk About

- Moving through five completely different careers without a map
- Why she kept leaving behind things she was good at
- Building Hastam in 2020 — the timing that felt like failure
- Making learning kits with her hands during lockdown
- The moment her son became her teacher
- What identity actually means when you've reinvented yourself five times
- Finding purpose in the most unexpected place
