## About This Episode

Arjun Warrier spent 14 years in the Indian Navy. Ten of those years were at sea — three theatre commands, night shifts on the bridge, a career that demanded everything. He was built for it. Trained for it. Defined by it.

Then came a dive. 30 metres down off Havelock Island, something shifted. It took him three more years to act on it.

What followed was not a clean exit but a deliberate, painful burial of an identity. The Navy officer, the uniform, the rank — all of it had to be unmade. And then, from zero, he rebuilt himself: IIM Lucknow, EY, Sri Lanka, and lessons in proximity over effort.

## What We Talk About

- The Navy SSB selection and the brutal 30-day body transformation that followed
- Ten years at sea, theatre commands, and the night he witnessed 40 warships under fighter jets
- The moment underwater that cracked everything open — and why it took three years to act
- "The burial" — the process of unbecoming who he was for 14 years
- Rebuilding after the Navy: business school, corporate life, and what he learned about real growth
- Why proximity to the right people matters more than just grinding hard
